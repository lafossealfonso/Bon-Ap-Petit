A TASTE OF FRANCE, A TALE OF THE UNBELIEVABLE.

Legend has it that Bon Ap’ Petit owes its existence to a forgotten rivalry between two chefs in 1920s Paris. Étienne Devereaux, an ambitious French cook, found himself in a heated debate with a visiting Belgian gourmand who claimed that true culinary genius lay not in delicate pastries or elaborate sauces, but in the mastery of simple, satisfying food. To prove his point, Étienne set out to elevate the humble mitraillette—a baguette stuffed with steak, fries, and sauce—into something worthy of the finest Parisian tables. After countless failed attempts, he perfected a version so balanced, so indulgent, that the Belgian took one bite, nodded in approval, and walked out without another word. Étienne, his mission complete, left Paris soon after, his recipe tucked away, bound for new shores.

Bon Ap’ Petit continues that legacy in the heart of Brunswick, blending French tradition with a touch of Melbourne’s inventive spirit. Our signature mitraillette sits proudly alongside bistro classics, each dish crafted with the same dedication to bold flavors and simple pleasures. Whether you’re here for a slow afternoon over wine and cheese or a hearty, satisfying meal, we welcome you to experience a little piece of France, shaped by an old challenge and perfected over time.
